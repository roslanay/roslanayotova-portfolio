3 Why did you choose this topic?

My starting point was proxemics which is the study of space in body language.
I was interested in how we embody and literary position ourselves in relation to others.

This is a System for the Notation of Proxemic Behavior which uses biometrics to conceptualise different categories and describes the ways in which we relate to others with our bodies in terms of space. 
 
Meanwhile looking for other notational systems to describe spatial relations I came across the architect’s data - a book that provides information about spatial requirements in building design and site planning. Apart from all the metric data that comes with it there are also the subtle movements of the figures which I find interesting. They illustrate social habits and the ways in which we are expected to interact with our immediate surroundings. They impose ways of embodiment. 



Naturally I was interested in peoples movement in public space. Big crowds of people are acting very much the same way as flocks of birds or schools of fish. Collective motion is defined as the spontaneous emergence of ordered movement in a system consisting of a large number of self-driven agents.

???????Architects can never have full control of the factors which exist in expendable, and developing forms.

I read some situationist urban critique who experimented, analysing the factors affecting their mood, behaviour, and choice of route as they wandered their "drift" through the city. Psychogeography is a term developed in order to define the journey of introspection within our environment and to challenge the ‘question of the construction and perception of urban space’. 


Aldo van Eyck was a Dutch architect and also the designer of 700 playgrounds which proposed a different conception of space and interaction.He designed the equipment in a very minimalist way, to stimulate the imagination of the children, the idea that they could appropriate the space by its openness to interpretation. 

Very inspired by the way he portrays movement in his floor plan drawings.
These dancing diagrams remind me of the maps and floor plans mentioned. The choreographer is the architect of movement. 
Thats how I got to the idea of architecture as choreography.
